Fire Alarm

Imagine you’ve just started your workday. You login and see a large number of emails from distraught customers. Clearly, something is happening. What steps do you take?

To start I would do a search for all email in the chain and see what information I could get from the last email in the chain. I would do a thorough scan of the email to see what steps have already been taken and what solutions, if any, have already been presented.
If it is still an ongoing issue I would contact the team member who last touched the issue to get a more in depth understanding of what is going and what steps have taken place to resolve the issue.

Step-by-Step

We’d like to get an idea of how you explain concepts and processes. What was the last thing your purchased online? Tell us, step-by-step, how you went about searching, choosing, and completing your purchase.

Recently I had to purchase some parts for my dishwasher to keep the top rack from falling whenever it was pulled out. Below are the steps that it took to be able to find and purchase the needed parts.

  1. Find the serial and model number of the dishwasher. This was located on the inside portion of the door on the left hand side.
  2. I took a photo of the model number tag and then searched online at the Kitchenaid website for manuals for the specific dishwasher I had.
  3. Once I found the manuals I downloaded the parts list for my dishwasher and looked through the pdf file to find the parts I needed. This was easy due to the drawings through out the manual that had numbers for each specific part.
  4. I found that number of the needed part, then cross referenced that number with the part number list.
  5. Once the part number was found I did a search on Amazon for Kitchenaid dishwasher parts using the specific model for the needed part. An example of my search is Kitchenaid W10195336 mount wheel which brought up a whirlpool part that was cpompatible with my disherwasher.
